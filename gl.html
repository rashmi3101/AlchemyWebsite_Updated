Workshop on Computational Fluid Dynamics

Have you ever wondered what makes a space shuttle fly indefinitely? A vortex swirl with high velocity? Propulsion of a Sea Vessel? Or how a Pneumatic cylinder works? ‘Fluid Mechanics’ gives us the answer. With the intervention of Technology, it is possible to simulate the above mentioned on a 2D screen. Welcome to the world of Computational Fluid Dynamics. CFD uses numerical methods and algorithms to solve and analyze problems that involve fluid flows. . We will help you walk besides the great scientists Navier, Reynolds, Euler, Stokes and rediscover their theories with the help of Technology. CFD (Ansys) provides excellent platform for young chemical engineering aspirants to learn, understand and simulate the problems in their textbooks, assisting, as a teacher, a guide and an expert. Be a part of the elite who controls the flow of fluids in an Industry and thus its economy.
  • Registration Fee: Rs. 300/- per participant.
  • Workshop Date and Duration: 15th March,2014. Two sessions (3+3 hrs).
  • Mandatory Requirements: Laptop and Charger.
  • In case a participant is unable to bring a laptop as mentioned by him/her in the registration form, they will be provided with one, subject to availability of the same. Best arrangements will be made to ensure that every one who has registered for the workshop has a laptop to work on.
  • Certificates will be provided.

    Delivered By Mr. M.Muthukrishnan , General Manager, R&D and Coal Research, Bharat Heavy Electricals Limited, Trichy
    Chief Guest (A2 Hall – 13th March, 4.30-5.30 pm)

    History has proved time and again that genius inspires genius. While talent is what it takes to hit a target no one else can it takes genius to hit a target that none can see. Here at Alchemy, we introduce you to some of the best thinkers of this era- men who have explored the edges of what is already known, and beyond.
    One such person is Mr. Muthukrishnan, who graduated in Chemical engineering from Madras University , Tamil Nadu and obtained Masters degree (M.S . by Research in Particle Technology) from IIT Chennai in 1981. He joined Bharat Heavy Electrics Ltd (BHEL), Trichy in 1981.
    He started his career in the Research and Development of Fluidised Bed Combustion (FBC) boilers and involved in spreading this technology up to 250MW capacity size in the country for utilizing low grade fuels by providing operational support and trouble shooting of BHEL supplied FBC boilers. He has headed the Engineering and Development group of FBC boilers, Heat Recovery Steam Generators (HRSG) and Industrial boilers in the Department of Process and Captive Power Systems (PCPS) at Tiruchi. Presently he is the head of Research & Development and Coal Research group as General Manager, R&D and Coal Research leading the team on Advanced Ultra Super critical Technology , Integrated Gasification and Combined Cycle (IGCC) projects, FBC Boiler Development besides extensive studies on coal combustion and characterization . So be present , get ready to imbibe knowledge from this maestro – and who knows? You might get inspired to be his replica in the years to come!!
